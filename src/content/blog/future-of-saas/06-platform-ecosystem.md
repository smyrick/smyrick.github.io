## What the Major Platforms Are Signaling

Across the market, major platforms are converging on a similar shape:

- a shared graph or context layer
- an integration ecosystem or marketplace
- an AI or agent layer on top
- a governance/control plane around all of it

## Salesforce

Salesforce is pushing toward:

- Data Cloud and metadata as the enterprise context layer
- Agentforce as orchestration over that layer
- marketplaces for apps, actions, and agent skills
- governance as part of enterprise credibility

The signal is clear: the moat is not just CRM screens. It is the graph plus governed action.

## Microsoft

Microsoft is using:

- Dataverse and Microsoft Graph as knowledge and data substrate
- Copilot and Copilot Studio as AI layer
- broad connector ecosystems and marketplaces
- policy, enforcement, and evidence as AI governance narrative

This is a strong example of “graph + governance + AI” in one stack.

## Atlassian

Atlassian is especially illustrative:

- Teamwork Graph as shared context
- Rovo as AI search and agent layer
- Forge and Marketplace as extension surface
- MCP support to make the graph useful to external agent tools

It is explicitly building for a world where many agents may want to use Atlassian data and workflows.

## ServiceNow

ServiceNow’s pattern is:

- a unified workflow and service graph
- Integration Hub for external systems
- Now Assist and AI agents on top
- AI Control Tower and governance at the center

This is almost a pure expression of the essay thesis.

## Datadog and Other Substrate Platforms

Even where the product is not framed as an agent platform, the same shape shows up.

Datadog’s moat is:

- integration-dense telemetry graph
- operational observability backbone
- extension ecosystem and marketplace
- growing relevance as monitoring substrate for AI systems themselves

## Strategic Pattern Across Them All

These platforms are not behaving as if AI replaces SaaS.

They are behaving as if:

- AI becomes a new client of the platform
- the platform’s durable value is the graph, ecosystem, and governance
- marketplaces and protocols determine distribution and composability

## Takeaway

The important competitive question is not who has the best standalone copilot.

It is who owns the most useful, trusted, and governable substrate that many agents will depend on.
