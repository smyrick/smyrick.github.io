## Why Stress-Test the Thesis

If the argument only works for CRM or support tooling, it is weak. It should also explain categories as different as observability, travel marketplaces, CI/CD, and cloud infrastructure.

## Datadog

### What the Cloneable Surface Looks Like

- dashboards
- ad hoc alerts
- simple telemetry views

### What the Moat Really Is

- dense integration catalog
- unified telemetry graph
- operational maturity and scale
- observability for complex distributed systems and now AI systems themselves

## Expedia

### Cloneable Surface

- trip planning UI
- recommendation layers
- booking flows at the front end

### Hard Moat

- supplier relationships
- transaction guarantees
- pricing/inventory complexity
- fraud, policy, and operational handling

## CircleCI

### Cloneable Surface

- basic CI workflows
- pipeline UI
- simple automation around build/test/deploy

### Hard Moat

- ecosystem of reusable building blocks
- reliable orchestration backbone
- permissions, artifacts, and workflow history
- organizational embed in delivery pipelines

## AWS

### Cloneable Surface

- some higher-level developer workflows
- wrapper UX around common infra tasks

### Hard Moat

- control plane depth
- IAM and policy fabric
- service breadth
- ecosystem and marketplace gravity
- role as substrate for other systems of record

## Constants Across All Four

- durable value sits in data, relationships, and guarantees
- integration ecosystems compound over time
- governance and operational maturity are central
- APIs are the real product surface for agents

## Variables Across Categories

- the nature of the system of record differs
- regulatory burden varies widely
- extensibility lives in different places
- some are agent hosts, some are agent targets, some are both

## Final Conclusion

The thesis largely holds across very different SaaS types.

AI increases pressure on surface-level experiences everywhere. But across these categories, the durable position still comes from owning trusted substrate: data, integrations, guarantees, and governed execution.

The practical implication is not that SaaS disappears. It is that more SaaS products are redefined as the systems underneath the agents rather than the only place work happens.
